Tiago Belintani

Researcher | Postdoctoral Fellow | Evolutionary Scientist – Brazil

I am a postdoctoral researcher at São Paulo State University (UNESP), exploring the evolutionary biology, systematics, and phylogenomics of key Neotropical organisms. My research focuses primarily on two fascinating and ecologically significant groups: the Triatominae (blood-feeding insects and vectors of Chagas disease) and the Theraphosidae (the often misunderstood tarantulas).

My academic path merges classical zoological training with modern bioinformatics and genomic analysis. From dusty entomological collections to high-throughput DNA sequencing, I aim to uncover hidden patterns of diversification, adaptation, and biogeography. I utilize tools such as geometric morphometrics, phylogenetic inference, and comparative genomics to trace evolutionary lineages and resolve complex species relationships.

I see science as both narrative and exploration. Every sequence, specimen, and cladogram contributes to a broader understanding of life’s tangled branches — and I map them with curiosity, rigor, and enthusiasm.
