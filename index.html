Tiago Belintani

Researcher | Postdoctoral Fellow | Evolutionary Scientist – Brazil

I am a postdoctoral researcher at São Paulo State University (UNESP), where I delve into the evolutionary biology, systematics, and phylogenomics of key Neotropical organisms. My research focuses primarily on two fascinating and ecologically significant groups: the Triatominae (blood-feeding insects and vectors of Chagas disease) and the Theraphosidae (the often misunderstood tarantulas).

My academic background merges classical training in zoology with modern bioinformatics and genomic analysis. From dusty entomological collections to high-throughput DNA sequencing, my work seeks to understand the hidden patterns of diversification, adaptation, and biogeography.
